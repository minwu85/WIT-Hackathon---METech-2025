What is AI and Deepfake?

ü§ñ Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Artificial Intelligence is technology that enables machines to simulate human intelligence. AI can learn patterns, make decisions, and create content that appears human-made.

üé≠ Deepfake Technology

Deepfakes use AI to create realistic but fake content by replacing someone's likeness with someone else's. This technology can generate:

  • Fake Videos: Making it appear someone said or did something they never did
  • Fake Images: Creating realistic photos of people, places, or events that never existed
  • Fake Voices: Mimicking someone's voice to say anything

‚öñÔ∏è Why It Matters

Concerns:

  • Spreading misinformation
  • Identity theft and fraud
  • Damaging reputations
  • Political manipulation

Positive Uses:

  • Movie special effects
  • Historical recreations
  • Language dubbing
  • Art and creativity

Case Study: The Viral Deepfake

üì∞ Real-World Example

In 2019, a deepfake video of Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg went viral. The fake video showed him making statements he never actually made about controlling people's data.

What Happened:

  • Artists created a convincing deepfake video as an art project
  • The video spread rapidly across social media platforms
  • Many people initially believed it was real
  • It sparked debates about deepfake policies on social platforms

The Impact:

  • Raised awareness about deepfake technology
  • Led to new content policies on social media
  • Highlighted the need for detection tools
  • Showed how quickly fake content can spread

How does deepfake abuse happen?

üîß 'Nudify' apps and AI tools

Many deepfakes targeting young people are created with AI apps that appear to remove clothing from photos ‚Äì turning everyday images into explicit material. These images can appear shockingly realistic, especially when viewed on small screens and shared quickly.

A person can be targeted even if they've never shared an explicit image. The image could be a regular selfie, a school photo, or a social media post. When misused, these apps can turn ordinary images into tools of humiliation, coercion, or blackmail.

Nudify apps are widely accessible, free or low cost, and often require no technical skill. They are increasingly marketed to younger users via social platforms and forums. This makes them not only dangerous, but also deceptively easy ‚Äì turning impulsive or careless decisions into serious harm.

üì± Fake images and videos

Once created, deepfake images and videos can be shared as supposedly 'harmless' fun, a form of bullying, or as part of deliberate image-based abuse.

Even when people know the content is fake, the emotional and psychological harm is very real ‚Äì especially for teens who may feel exposed or powerless.

Some students are tricked or threatened using these fakes. Others are sent AI-generated content of their peers, causing distress, confusion, and gossip. Young people experiment without understanding the serious legal and personal consequences.

Who is affected?

üéØ Targets

Any student or school staff member can be targeted, regardless of what they post online or how they behave. All it takes is one image, the wrong app and someone who either recklessly or knowingly causes harm.

Targets often experience intense emotional fallout: humiliation, fear, anger, confusion. Some are scared to speak up, fearing they won't be believed or will be blamed for what happened. They often also feel ashamed, even though they've done nothing wrong.

In some cases, the mere threat of a deepfake ‚Äì even if no fake exists ‚Äì is enough to cause distress or manipulate behaviour.

üë• Bystanders

Young people may also witness this behaviour ‚Äì in group chats, on social media, or via private messages. They may feel unsure whether to report it, what to say, or how to support their friends. They may also worry they'll be next.

‚ö†Ô∏è Creators

Some young people use these tools as a prank or experiment without fully understanding the impact. They may not realise that creating or sharing fake nudes, even synthetic ones, can be a serious criminal offence in some states and territories.

What does the law say?

‚öñÔ∏è Understanding the law

Understanding the law can help when you're educating young people or supporting someone who has experienced image-based abuse.

However, legal action is only one part of the solution; it cannot address everything on its own.

In some states, it is now a criminal offence to create or share AI-generated explicit content without consent, even if the material is synthetic.

For example, in South Australia, students as young as 16 can be prosecuted for creating or sharing humiliating or degrading deepfakes.

üìû Where to report

  • To your school - if the incident involves other students.
  • To local police - especially if a crime may have occurred.
  • To eSafety - using the Report Abuse portal. Include the police event number if available.
  • Never share or save explicit content - instead, make a written record of links (URLS), account names, and descriptions for evidence.

Health Scams & Deepfake Safety

üè• How Deepfakes Are Used in Health Scams

In December 2024, Diabetes Victoria exposed deepfake videos showing fake experts from The Baker Heart and Diabetes Institute promoting diabetes supplements. These convincing fake videos used real doctors' images without permission to sell unproven products.

This isn't isolated - scammers regularly use deepfake images of trusted doctors like Dr Karl Kruszelnicki to sell pills via social media. Even when reported, many platforms don't remove these deceptive ads.

üîç What to Look Out For

When viewing health-related content online, check for these warning signs:

  • Visual Issues: Blurring, pixelation, or skin discoloration
  • Audio Problems: Badly synced sound or unnatural speech patterns
  • Context Clues: Ask yourself - would this person really say this?
  • Movement: Irregular blinking or unnatural gestures
  • Background: Inconsistent lighting or sudden changes

Verify the Source

Contact the person directly to confirm if the endorsement is legitimate

Question Publicly

Leave comments questioning suspicious claims to help others be critical

Report Fake Content

Use platform reporting tools to flag fake products and misinformation

Consult Professionals

Always seek advice from qualified healthcare providers before making health decisions
